About Me

I’m currently a junior at Georgia Tech, majoring in Electrical Engineering with a special interest in nanoelectronics and emerging technologies. My journey in science and engineering started early, fueled by curiosity about how things work at both the macro and nano scale.

With hands-on experience in research labs and hardware design projects, I aim to push boundaries and create solutions that can positively impact society. I’m especially fascinated by SOT-MRAM memory systems, novel device fabrication, and exploring how technology can shape our world.

In the future, I hope to pursue deeper research opportunities, continue innovating, and share my knowledge with others.

Experience

SOT-MRAM Research (Undergraduate Research Assistant)

As an undergraduate research assistant in the Nanoelectronics Research Lab at Georgia Tech, I investigate cutting-edge Spin-Orbit Torque Magnetic Random Access Memory (SOT-MRAM). My work spans iterative simulations in MATLAB and COMSOL, exploring ways to optimize performance metrics like energy efficiency and data retention. This experience has sharpened both my technical and interdisciplinary communication skills while contributing to next-gen memory technology.

Product Engineer Intern at NanoPrintek

As a Product Engineer Intern at NanoPrintek, I performed hands-on soldering and wiring for the company’s dry printing technology, helping to optimize system design and enhance printing precision. I created technical manuals and instructional videos to guide end-users, significantly reducing setup-related inquiries. I also represented the startup at a major industry conference, showcasing product features and securing interest from new partners in expanding the applications of dry printing.

Projects

FPGA External Memory Project

In my Digital System Design Lab, I developed proficiency in VHDL coding, FPGA programming, and hardware debugging. I built a secure external memory peripheral for SCOMP, supporting stack and queue operations, password protection, and more. This hands-on experience taught me meticulous testing and step-by-step troubleshooting, honing my analytical and problem-solving skills.

MediBee Tracker

For a Grand Challenges Living Learning Community project, my team and I created a tracking device/database concept to facilitate medical resource delivery after natural disasters. I developed the prototype using GPS modules, LoRa antennas, an Arduino Nano, and a fingerprint sensor for authentication. Overcoming hardware hurdles taught me the value of thorough testing and creativity in problem-solving.
